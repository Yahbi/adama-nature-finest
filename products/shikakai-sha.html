Soapnut's soft foam, honey's slip; lengths cleansed without the squeak.

A gentle Ayurvedic-inspired cleanser that softly foams with shikakai and soapnut, with honey — cleanses without stripping.

How to use
Lather into wet hair, massage through, then rinse.
  • The fruit shell of Sapindus mukorossi is recognised for its triterpenoid saponins — of the oleanane, dammarane and tirucallane types. Saponins are plant surfactants: amphiphilic molecules that lower surface tension and foam in water.1
  • A saponin fraction purified from the soapnut shell was resolved by HPLC-MS into four oleanane-type triterpenoid saponins. The saponins are a fact of the shell; the seed oil is a different material and is not used here.2
  • The pod of Acacia concinna — shikakai — is recognised for a saponin mixture built on acacic acid, a triterpene aglycone, carrying a monoterpene unit and five sugars: glucose, xylose, rhamnose, quinovose and arabinose.3
  • Alkaline hydrolysis of the shikakai pod's polar saponin fraction yields the prosapogenols concinnosides A–F, alongside the known glycosides acaciaside, julibroside A1, julibroside A3 and albiziasaponin C.4
  • The inner leaf of Aloe vera is recognised for its acetylated polysaccharide fraction, reported at 3.82–6.55 g/kg of the gel, with phenolic acids and flavonoids. The gel and the leaf latex are different materials; it is the gel that is used here.7
  • Aloe vera has a long record of use in cosmetology; the dermatological literature that describes that record also states plainly that controlled trials remain wanting.8

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Triterpenoid saponins — oleanane, dammarane and tirucallane types
The principal constituent class of the soapnut fruit shell. Sapindoside A and sapindoside B are among the oleanane-type saponins described.
Acacic-acid triterpenoid saponins
The dominant constituent class of the shikakai pod, glycosylated with glucose, xylose, rhamnose, quinovose and arabinose.
Concinnosides A–F
Prosapogenols isolated from the shikakai pod saponin fraction.
Acetylated polysaccharides
The polysaccharide fraction of the Aloe vera inner leaf gel.
Coco-Glucoside and Sodium Cocoyl Isethionate
The two non-sulphate surfactants; they carry the cleansing.
Mel (Honey)
A bee product, and the only animal-derived material in the formula.
Glycerin
Humectant.

Full ingredients — INCI

Aqua, Coco-Glucoside, Sodium Cocoyl Isethionate, Acacia Concinna Fruit Extract, Mel (Honey), Sapindus Mukorossi Fruit Extract, Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice, Glycerin, Sodium Benzoate.

Vessel
200 mL pump bottle
Recyclability
Pump mechanisms combine several materials and a metal spring, and are not accepted by most kerbside streams. Separate the pump from the bottle before disposal.

Storage. Keep the pump closed. Store away from direct sunlight and heat.

Precautions. Two published records bear on Acacia Concinna Fruit Extract and we state them rather than omit them. The Cosmetic Ingredient Review expert panel concluded that the available data were insufficient to support the safety of the ingredient in cosmetics, naming skin sensitisation and contact urticaria among the gaps. A 2025 case report describes occupational IgE-mediated respiratory allergy in a person handling shikakai powder — a single case, by inhalation rather than skin contact. Honey is a bee product. Avoid the eyes. Discontinue if irritation occurs.
