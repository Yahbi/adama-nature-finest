How to use
Apply to clean skin, morning and night.
  • Oat is recognised for its avenanthramides — N-cinnamoylanthranilic acids characteristic of the grain — and for a beta-glucan fraction reported significantly higher in oat than in buckwheat. A systematic review of 154 studies catalogued 113 bioactive compounds in oat; extraction methods differ across those studies, so quantities are ranges rather than fixed values.1
  • The grain also carries steroidal saponins. Avenacosides A and B were quantified across fifteen commercial oat products at 49.6–443.0 mg/kg total.2
  • Shea butter is recognised for triacylglycerols dominated by stearic and oleic acids, and for a fraction of acetyl and cinnamyl polycyclic triterpene esters reported at country means of 3.7–12.6% of the fat. That balance shifts with provenance: stearic acid predominates in West African shea, oleic acid in Ugandan.3

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Ceramide NP
A ceramide of the non-hydroxy-acyl phytosphingosine class.
Avena Sativa Kernel Extract
Oat kernel extract. The grain's characteristic constituents are avenanthramides, beta-glucans and steroidal saponins.
Panthenol
The alcohol analogue of pantothenic acid — vitamin B5.
Butyrospermum Parkii Butter
Shea butter. The INCI name retains the synonym Butyrospermum parkii; the accepted botanical name is Vitellaria paradoxa C.F.Gaertn. Stearic- and oleic-acid-dominant triacylglycerols with a polycyclic triterpene ester fraction.

Full ingredients — INCI

Aqua, Glycerin, Butyrospermum Parkii Butter, Cetearyl Alcohol, Ceramide NP, Avena Sativa Kernel Extract, Panthenol, Xanthan Gum, Benzyl Alcohol, Dehydroacetic Acid.

Vessel
50 mL jar.

Storage. Close the jar after use. Keep away from direct light and heat.

Precautions. For external use only. Avoid the eye area. Oat is a cereal, and contact sensitisation to oat-derived cosmetic ingredients is documented; anyone with a known oat or cereal sensitivity should treat this formula accordingly. Discontinue use if irritation occurs.
