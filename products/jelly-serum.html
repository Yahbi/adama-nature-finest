How to use
Apply two to three drops to clean skin, morning and night, before oil or cream.
  • Aloe's inner leaf gel is recognised for its polysaccharide fraction — polysaccharides, reported at 3.82–6.55 g/kg — chiefly acetylated mannans — together with phenolic acids and flavonoids. The gel carries the majority of the plant's reported bioactive compounds; the review notes that further controlled trials are needed to substantiate benefit.1
  • Reviews of the plant's phytochemistry establish polysaccharides and flavonoids among its principal constituent classes, alongside vitamins, minerals and amino acids.2
  • Aloe has been used in cosmetology for a long time and is described as such in the dermatological literature, which states plainly that controlled trials are still required to determine real efficacy.3

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Inulin
A fructan — a chain of fructose units drawn from plant storage tissue.
Sodium hyaluronate
The sodium salt of hyaluronic acid; a glycosaminoglycan that binds water.
Niacinamide
The amide of vitamin B3; water-soluble.
Aloe barbadensis leaf juice
The pressed inner leaf. Its constituent fraction is polysaccharide — chiefly acetylated mannans — with phenolic acids and flavonoids. Cosmetic aloe is inner-leaf material, distinct from the leaf latex.
Alpha-glucan oligosaccharide
A short glucose-linked oligosaccharide.
Glycerin
A polyol humectant.
Xanthan gum
A fermentation-derived polysaccharide; it gives the serum its gel.
Sodium benzoate, potassium sorbate
The preservative pair. A water-based formula requires one.

Full ingredients — INCI

Aqua, Glycerin, Inulin, Sodium Hyaluronate, Niacinamide, Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice, Alpha-Glucan Oligosaccharide, Xanthan Gum, Sodium Benzoate, Potassium Sorbate.

Vessel
30 mL opaque stoneware dropper bottle with wood collar
Material
Stoneware body; rubber bulb and glass pipette
Recyclability
The stoneware, rinsed, is made to be kept and refilled. The pipette assembly separates from the body; remove it before disposal.

Storage. Keep the bottle closed, upright, away from direct sunlight and heat. The opaque stoneware limits light exposure; the preservative system does the rest.

Precautions. For external use. Avoid the eye area. Discontinue if irritation occurs. Aloe is documented as an occasional contact sensitiser; patch test on the inner forearm if your skin is reactive.
