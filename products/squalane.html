Olive Squalane

$34 100 mL

Olive-derived, weightless, identical to what young skin makes for itself.

Skin-identical squalane derived from olives. Weightless, scentless; the emollient every other formula borrows, offered straight.

Key ingredients
  • Olive Squalane
How to use
Two to four drops any time; layers under or over anything without conflict.
Botanical name
Olea europaea L.
Family
Oleaceae
Part used
Fruit
Function
Emollient
Presentation
100 mL, dropper bottle
Also known as
Olive, Olivier, European olive, Zaytoun / Zeitoun (Arabic), Azemmur (Amazigh)
How to use
Two to four drops, morning or night. Apply to clean skin alone, or layer with other preparations in any order.
Dosage
Used neat. There is no dilution to observe.
In practice
A single-constituent material: the bottle contains squalane and nothing else. It is miscible with plant oils and compatible with water-based preparations applied before or after it, so it does not need to hold a fixed position in a routine. It may also be used to dilute a more concentrated oil.

Principal constituents

Squalane (C30H62)
The sole constituent. A saturated aliphatic hydrocarbon — the fully hydrogenated form of squalene. Because it carries no carbon-carbon double bonds, it is not a fatty acid, a triglyceride, or an oil in the ordinary sense; it is one molecule, isolated from the olive, and the material is that molecule alone.

Full ingredients — INCI

Squalane.

Vessel
Dropper bottle, 100 mL
Material
Stoneware bottle; dropper assembly of glass pipette, wooden collar and rubber bulb
Recyclability
The stoneware, rinsed, is made to be kept and refilled. The dropper assembly is mixed-material and should be separated from the bottle and discarded.

Storage. Store closed, away from direct light and heat.

Precautions. For external use. Avoid contact with the eyes. Discontinue use if irritation occurs.
