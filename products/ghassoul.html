Ghassoul Clay

$19 200 g

Washed clay from the Atlas; hair and body, as the hammams use it.

Mined in the Middle Atlas, sun-dried, milled — the clay the hammams have washed with for centuries. Cleanses hair and skin without a single surfactant.

Key ingredients
  • Moroccan Lava Clay
How to use
Mix two spoons with warm water into a paste; hair, face or body, then rinse.
Botanical name
Stevensite — (Ca,Na)x(Mg,Fe)3Si4O10(OH)2·nH2O, a magnesium-rich trioctahedral smectite
Function
Mineral clay, milled to a powder; mixed with water for hair and body.
Presentation
Powder, 200 g
Also known as
Ghassoul, Rhassoul, Moroccan stevensite, Moroccan lava clay, غاسول
How to use
Mix two spoons with warm water into a paste. Apply to hair, face or body, then rinse.
Dosage
Roughly two spoons of clay to enough warm water to make a paste that holds its shape.
In practice
Ghassoul is the clay of the Moroccan hammam, used there on hair and body long before any of it was written down. It is worked with water and nothing else: no surfactant, no emulsifier, no preservative. Mix only what you intend to use; the paste is made at the basin, not stored.
  • Ghassoul is a magnesium-rich stevensite — a trioctahedral smectite clay, its magnesium held in the octahedral sheet between silicate layers.1
  • The mineral is characterised as essentially stevensite, with calcite, dolomite and quartz as accessory phases.2
  • It is a swelling clay: the interlayer takes up water and expands, and holds exchangeable cations — sodium, potassium, calcium, magnesium — that it can trade with the water around it.3
  • Clay minerals as a class are used in cosmetic formulation as absorbents and as rheology modifiers.4

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Stevensite
The dominant mineral phase — a magnesium-rich trioctahedral smectite.
Magnesium
The principal octahedral cation of the clay layer.
Silicon
The tetrahedral silicate sheets that sandwich the octahedral layer.
Exchangeable interlayer cations
Sodium, potassium, calcium and magnesium, held between the layers and exchangeable with the surrounding water.
Interlayer water
Water held between the silicate layers; the clay swells as it takes more up.
Calcite, dolomite, quartz
Accessory mineral phases reported alongside stevensite in Ghassoul.

Full ingredients — INCI

Moroccan Lava Clay.

Vessel
Stoneware jar, 200 g

Storage. Keep sealed and dry. The clay swells with water; any moisture that reaches the powder is taken into it and will not leave.

Precautions. For external use. Mix gently to avoid raising dust, and do not inhale the powder. Avoid the eyes; rinse with water if the paste enters them. Rinse thoroughly from skin and hair.
