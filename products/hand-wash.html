Botanical Hand Wash

$33 200 mL

Orange peel and aloe at the basin; a small ceremony, ten times a day.

A gently foaming plant-based hand wash that cleanses without drying — a small daily pleasure at every basin.

How to use
Lather over wet hands, then rinse.
  • Sweet orange peel oil is recognised for a composition dominated by a single monoterpene — d-limonene, reported between 8.84 and 30.72 per cent of the oil, the figure moving with the method of extraction.1
  • Aloe leaf juice is recognised for the polysaccharide fraction of the inner leaf gel — acetylated mannans, reported at 3.82 to 6.55 g/kg — which carries the majority of the constituents described for the plant.2
  • Aloe has long been used in cosmetic preparation; the dermatological literature records that use plainly, and states equally plainly that controlled trials are still wanting.3

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

d-Limonene
The principal volatile of sweet orange peel oil, reported at 8.84–30.72% of the oil depending on extraction method.
Acetylated polysaccharides (acemannan-type)
The characteristic fraction of the aloe inner leaf gel; reported at 3.82–6.55 g/kg of gel.
Coco-glucoside
An alkyl polyglucoside surfactant, condensed from coconut-derived fatty alcohols and glucose. The surfactant is what lifts oil into water.
Glycerin
A polyol humectant; it holds water.
Panthenol
The alcohol analogue of pantothenic acid.

Full ingredients — INCI

Aqua, Coco-Glucoside, Glycerin, Citrus Aurantium Dulcis Peel Oil, Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice, Panthenol, Sodium Benzoate, Potassium Sorbate, Citric Acid, Limonene.

Declarable fragrance allergens

Limonene

Vessel
200 mL pump bottle.
Recyclability
The pump is a mixed-material component. Remove it from the bottle and dispose of the two separately.

Storage. Close the pump after use. Keep from direct sun and heat.

Precautions. For external use. Avoid the eyes; rinse with water if it enters them. Limonene, a constituent of the orange peel oil, is one of the fragrance substances declarable under EC 1223/2009 and is listed on the ingredient deck; discontinue use if irritation appears.
