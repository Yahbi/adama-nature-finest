How to use
Massage into wet hair, work to a lather, rinse. Follow with Glossing Conditioner.
  • The flowers of Matricaria chamomilla are recognised for carrying the sesquiterpene lactone matricin and the terpene alcohol α-bisabolol, alongside apigenin-related flavonoids and luteolin derivatives — the constituent profile that distinguishes German chamomile from Roman.1
  • Chamomile flower is recognised for its phenolic fraction — apigenin, quercetin, luteolin and their glucosides — reported alongside the volatile terpenoids α-bisabolol and its oxides. The proportions vary with plant part, origin, season and method of extraction.2
  • The inner leaf of Aloe vera is recognised for its acetylated polysaccharide fraction, reported at 3.82–6.55 g/kg of the gel, together with phenolic acids and flavonoids. The gel is a different material from the leaf latex, and it is the gel that is used here.4
  • Aloe vera has a long record of use in cosmetology; the dermatological literature that describes it also states plainly that controlled trials remain wanting.5

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

α-Bisabolol and bisabolol oxides A and B
Terpene alcohols of the chamomile flower.
Matricin
The flower's sesquiterpene lactone. It is matricin that converts to chamazulene under steam distillation — an oil fact, not a fact of this infusion.
Apigenin and apigenin-7-O-glucoside
The principal flavonoids reported in the flower, with luteolin derivatives and quercetin.
Acetylated polysaccharides
The polysaccharide fraction of the Aloe vera inner leaf gel.
Coco-Glucoside and Sodium Cocoyl Isethionate
The two non-sulphate surfactants; they carry the cleansing.
Panthenol
Provitamin B5.
Glycerin
Humectant.

Full ingredients — INCI

Aqua, Coco-Glucoside, Sodium Cocoyl Isethionate, Glycerin, Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice, Panthenol, Chamomilla Recutita Extract, Citric Acid, Sodium Benzoate, Potassium Sorbate, Parfum.

Vessel
200 mL pump bottle
Recyclability
Pump mechanisms combine several materials and a metal spring, and are not accepted by most kerbside streams. Separate the pump from the bottle before disposal.

Storage. Keep the pump closed. Store away from direct sunlight and heat.

Precautions. Chamomile is an Asteraceae plant, and the family is a documented contact sensitiser; those sensitive to mugwort, ragweed or daisy may cross-react. Avoid the eyes. Discontinue if irritation occurs.
