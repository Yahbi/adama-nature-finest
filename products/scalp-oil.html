Rosemary Scalp Strengthening Oil

Rosemary and peppermint wake the scalp before washing; a cool prickle, then calm.

How to use
Part dry hair and apply to the scalp, section by section. Massage two to three minutes. Leave thirty minutes, or overnight, then shampoo.
Dosage
A few drops per parting; enough to wet the scalp, not the length.
  • Rosemary leaf oil is a monoterpene oil. Roughly 150 compounds have been reported across samples of it, of which 1,8-cineole, α-pinene and camphor are the most frequently recorded.1
  • In one GC/MS analysis of a single rosemary leaf oil, 1,8-cineole was measured at 28.43% and α-pinene at 26.62%. Rosemary runs to distinct chemotypes — cineole, camphor, verbenone — and these figures describe that one sample, not the species.2
  • Rosemary is reputed to hold an antioxidant role in skin. A 2023 review of the in-vitro, animal and human literature concludes that large controlled clinical trials are still required before any clinical effect can be claimed.3
  • Jojoba is not an oil. Its seed lipid reserve is liquid wax — long-chain fatty acids esterified to long-chain fatty alcohols — rather than the triacylglycerols every other seed in this formula carries.4
  • In one analysis, the fatty acid moieties of those jojoba wax esters were eicosenoic acid at 55.50%, erucic acid at 20.43% and oleic acid at 19.01%.5
  • Jojoba wax has long been used in topical formulation and in dermatological preparations; the evidence surveyed for it remains largely preclinical.6

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

1,8-Cineole and α-pinene
The two constituents most consistently reported at the front of rosemary leaf oil.
Camphor
Among the three most frequently reported constituents of rosemary leaf oil across the literature.
Jojoba wax esters
Long-chain fatty acid and fatty alcohol esters — the dominant fraction of the jojoba seed lipid, and a liquid wax rather than a triglyceride.
Eicosenoic, erucic and oleic acids
The principal fatty acid moieties of the jojoba wax esters.
Tocopherol
Vitamin E, present in the formula.

Full ingredients — INCI

Ricinus Communis Seed Oil, Simmondsia Chinensis Seed Oil, Sesamum Indicum Seed Oil, Rosmarinus Officinalis Leaf Oil, Mentha Piperita Oil, Tocopherol, Limonene.

Declarable fragrance allergens

Limonene

Vessel
Dropper bottle, 50 mL
Material
Stoneware bottle; dropper assembly of glass pipette and rubber bulb.
Recyclability
The stoneware, rinsed, is made to be kept and refilled. The dropper assembly mixes materials; separate it from the bottle before disposal.

Storage. Closed, away from light and heat.

Precautions. For external use, on the scalp. Avoid the eyes. The formula contains essential oils of rosemary and peppermint; rosemary leaf oil is a cineole- and camphor-bearing material, and topical safe-use levels for it are a matter for a formulation and IFRA review rather than for this page. Limonene is present and is declared on the ingredient list as a fragrance allergen under EC 1223/2009. Do not apply to broken or inflamed skin. Use during pregnancy and on children is not established here; take medical advice.
