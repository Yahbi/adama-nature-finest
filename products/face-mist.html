Hydrating Face Mist

Rose water and aloe, fine as sea fog; the afternoon, reset.

A fine botanical mist — a reset for face and mind, whenever it needs it.

Also known as
Damask rose, Rose de Damas, Gol-e-Mohammadi, Kazanlak rose, Rose water, Rose hydrosol
How to use
Mist over clean skin, or at any point through the day. Hold roughly 20 cm from the face. Avoid the eyes.
  • Damask rose is recognised in the ethnopharmacological literature for its distilled preparations. The review that records this identifies citronellol and geraniol as the principal constituents of the essential oil, and describes the activity attributed to the plant as largely traditional and preclinical rather than demonstrated in people.1
  • By analogy with the essential oil — whose citronellol content has been reported to shift with growing site, soil and irrigation water, reaching roughly 29 per cent at one site and considerably less at another — no rose distillate is quite the same twice.2
  • The inner leaf of aloe is recognised for carrying the majority of the plant's reported bioactive compounds, among them acetylated polysaccharides — reported at 3.82 to 6.55 g/kg of the gel — alongside phenolic acids and flavonoids.3
  • soften and moisten skin — a use long enough established to have its own dermatological review, which states in the same breath that controlled trials are still needed to determine real efficacy.4

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Rosa Damascena Flower Water
The aqueous fraction of the petal distillation, first on the list. 2-phenylethanol is the constituent reported as principal to this water-soluble fraction — distinct from the citronellol and geraniol that carry into the oil.
Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice
Inner leaf gel. The polysaccharide fraction, not the leaf latex — the anthraquinones of the latex are a separate material and are not what is used here.
Glycerin
A polyol humectant.
Inulin
A plant fructan — a chain of fructose units. It is the ingredient behind the word prebiotic. Our bibliography carries nothing on it; it is stated here as a composition fact and nothing more.
Panthenol
Provitamin B5, the alcohol analogue of pantothenic acid.

Full ingredients — INCI

Rosa Damascena Flower Water, Aqua, Glycerin, Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice, Inulin, Panthenol, Sodium Benzoate, Potassium Sorbate, Citric Acid.

Vessel
100 mL opaque stoneware cylinder with an atomiser.
Material
Stoneware body; phenolic atomiser with a dip tube.
Recyclability
The stoneware is fired clay — kept and refilled rather than recycled. The atomiser is a mixed assembly of plastic and a metal spring; separate it from the bottle before disposal.

Storage. Store closed, away from direct light and heat. The stoneware is opaque, keeping light from the contents.

Precautions. For external use. Avoid the eye area. Discontinue if irritation occurs. Preserved with sodium benzoate and potassium sorbate in a base acidified with citric acid — the pair requires an acid base to function, so do not dilute or decant.
