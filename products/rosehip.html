In practice
One ingredient, unblended. The published work on Rosa canina is compositional: it establishes what is in the material, and the antioxidant figures reported for the oil are in-vitro radical-scavenging measurements, not measurements on skin. We state the constituents and leave the inference to you. Two distinctions worth holding. The seed oil and the hip itself are different materials from the same plant — the vitamin C, anthocyanins and polyphenols belong to the hip flesh, not to a pressed seed oil, and this document describes only the latter. And much of what is sold as rosehip oil, or as Rosa Mosqueta, is Rosa rubiginosa or Rosa moschata; the literature below is for Rosa canina, which is what this is.
  • A polyunsaturated seed oil. Linoleic acid predominates and α-linolenic acid is present in quantity — the material is classed among the high-linolenic plant oils.2
  • Tocopherols — vitamin E — reported at up to approximately 1,125 mg/kg in analyses of the seed oil.1
  • Phytosterols, β-sitosterol among them, reported at up to approximately 6,485 mg/kg in analyses of the seed oil.1
  • Carotenoids reported at up to approximately 108 mg/kg in analyses of the seed oil — the pigments that account for its colour.1
  • Phenolic compounds, predominantly p-coumaric acid methyl ester, reported at up to approximately 784 µg/kg in the seed oil.1

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Linoleic acid
The predominant fatty acid of the seed oil.
α-Linolenic acid
Present in quantity; the oil is classed as a high-linolenic oil.
Oleic acid
A monounsaturated fatty acid; a minor fraction of the seed oil, which is dominated by its polyunsaturates.
Tocopherols
α-, γ- and δ-; reported up to approximately 1,125 mg/kg in the seed oil.
Phytosterols
β-sitosterol among them; reported up to approximately 6,485 mg/kg.
Carotenoids
Reported up to approximately 108 mg/kg.
Phenolic compounds
Predominantly p-coumaric acid methyl ester; reported up to approximately 784 µg/kg.
Squalene
A triterpene hydrocarbon of the oil's unsaponifiable fraction, present in small amount.

Material

Recyclability
The stoneware, rinsed, is made to be kept and refilled. The dropper assembly is a mixed-material part: separate it from the bottle, and dispose of it as general waste.

Storage. Sealed, upright, away from light and heat. A high-linolenic oil oxidises readily; the opaque stoneware is part of the answer, and a closed bottle is the rest.

Precautions. External use only. Avoid the eye area. Conduct a patch test on the inner forearm before first use and discontinue if irritation occurs. Keep out of reach of children. Not intended to diagnose, treat or prevent any condition.
