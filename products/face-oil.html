How to use
Warm three or four drops between the palms and press into the skin as the last step of the evening routine. For external use.
  • Rose hip seed oil is reported to be a source of polyunsaturated fatty acids, linoleic and α-linolenic among them.1
  • Alongside those fatty acids, Rosa canina seed oil is reported to carry phytosterols, tocopherols and carotenoids, together with a small phenolic fraction — predominantly p-coumaric acid methyl ester.2
  • Jojoba is not an oil in the ordinary sense. Its seed reserve is liquid wax ester — long-chain fatty acids esterified to long-chain fatty alcohols — rather than the triacylglycerols of the two rose oils here.3
  • The acid moieties of the jojoba esters are principally eicosenoic, erucic and oleic; tocopherols and phytosterols accompany them.4
  • Jojoba wax has long been used in dermatological and cosmetic formulation, on the strength of that wax-ester structure.5

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Rose hip oil (Rosa canina, seed)
Reported to carry linoleic and α-linolenic acid, with tocopherols, phytosterols and carotenoids. The constituents named are those of the seed oil; the hip flesh is a different material with a different chemistry, and nothing here is claimed for it.
Squalane
A saturated hydrocarbon carried as the emollient base. No verified literature is held for this material; it therefore carries no property statement above.
Jojoba wax esters (Simmondsia chinensis, seed)
Liquid wax ester, not a triglyceride. Simmondsia chinensis stands alone in its own family, Simmondsiaceae.
Sea buckthorn oil (Hippophae rhamnoides)
No verified literature is held for this material; it therefore carries no property statement above.
Rose musk oil (Rosa moschata, seed)
A second Rosa species, declared separately from Rosa canina because the two are not interchangeable. No verified literature is held for R. moschata; it therefore carries no property statement above, and the Rosa canina literature is not extended to it.
Tocopherol
Added to the oil phase against oxidation.

Full ingredients — INCI

Rosa Canina Fruit Oil, Squalane, Simmondsia Chinensis Seed Oil, Hippophae Rhamnoides Oil, Rosa Moschata Seed Oil, Tocopherol, Parfum, Limonene, Linalool, Geraniol.

Declarable fragrance allergens

Limonene, Linalool, Geraniol

Vessel
Dropper bottle, 30 mL, with collar and bulb.
Material
Stoneware body; the dropper assembly is a separate part.
Recyclability
The stoneware is fired clay — kept and refilled rather than recycled. Separate the dropper from the bottle before disposal — the assembly is a mixed part.

Storage. Keep closed, upright, away from light and heat. Plant oils oxidise; the opaque stoneware slows it, the cap does the rest.

Precautions. External use only. Avoid the eye area. Contains the declarable fragrance allergens limonene, linalool and geraniol. Patch test before first use.
