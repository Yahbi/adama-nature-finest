Rose Hydrosol

$24 200 mL

The water that rises with the oil at distillation; Damask roses, one harvest.

The aromatic water carried up with Damask rose oil at distillation — one harvest, one still. Scents nothing artificially.

An aromatic water and its preservative pair. Nothing else. 200 mL.
Also known as
Damask rose, Rose de Damas, Gol-e-Mohammadi, Kazanlak rose, Rosa damascena Miller, Rose water, Rose hydrosol
Suited to
A leave-on facial mist. Read the precaution below before first use.
How to use
Mist over clean skin morning and night, or at any point the day needs resetting. Hold roughly 20 cm from the face. Avoid the eyes.
Dosage
Undiluted, as it comes.
In practice
An aromatic water, so it goes on before anything oily and after nothing. It will sit under a facial oil or a cream; it will not sit over one. Away from the face it is the liquid for a clay — mixed into kaolin or ghassoul in place of tap water, spoon for spoon.
  • Damask rose is recognised in the ethnopharmacological literature for its distilled preparations, of which the aromatic water is one. The review that records this identifies citronellol and geraniol as the principal constituents of the essential oil, and describes the activity attributed to the plant as largely traditional and preclinical rather than demonstrated in people.1
  • By analogy with the essential oil, which rises in the same still and has been profiled to over a hundred identified compounds: the distillate divides between an odorous terpene-alcohol fraction led by citronellol and a paraffin fraction of long-chain alkanes. The alkanes stay with the oil. What carries into the water is the water-soluble part of that same distillation.2
  • By analogy with the essential oil again — whose citronellol content has been reported to shift with growing site, soil and irrigation water, reaching roughly 29 per cent at one site and considerably less at another — no rose distillate is quite the same twice. We state the range rather than a specification.3
  • Rose water has a long record of traditional use on the skin. That record is what it is: a record of use. The strongest mechanistic work available concerns the polyphenolic residue left in the still after hydrodistillation — a different material from this one — and it was done in cultured fibroblasts, not on a face. Nothing in this literature establishes a soothing, toning or anti-ageing effect in human skin, and we do not assert one.4

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

2-Phenylethanol
Reported as the principal constituent of the rose-water fraction — the water-soluble half of the distillation, and the reason a hydrosol is not a diluted essential oil.
Phenylethyl acetate
The corresponding ester, reported alongside it.
Citronellol
The most abundant terpene alcohol of the distilled essential oil. Reported across published analyses at roughly 12 to 48 per cent of that oil, depending on origin and chemotype. It is an oil figure, not a water figure.
Geraniol
Named with citronellol as a principal constituent of the essential oil; reported at about 6.9 per cent in one analysis.
Nerol and linalool
Further terpene alcohols of the distillate.

Full ingredients — INCI

Rosa Damascena Flower Water, Sodium Benzoate, Potassium Sorbate.

Vessel
200 mL opaque stoneware cylinder with an atomiser.
Material
Stoneware body; atomiser with a dip tube.
Recyclability
The stoneware is fired clay — kept and refilled rather than recycled. The atomiser is a mixed assembly of plastic and a metal spring; separate it from the bottle before disposal.

Storage. Closed, upright, away from direct light and heat. The stoneware is opaque, keeping light from the contents.

Precautions. For external use. Avoid the eye area. Discontinue if irritation occurs. A rose distillate carries the plant's terpene alcohols — citronellol, geraniol and linalool are three of the twenty-six fragrance materials the European Union requires to be declared on a label above a set threshold; below it, they are not declared, and ours are not. That they are undeclared means they are below the threshold, not that they are absent. Anyone who has reacted to rose or to these materials before should patch test on the inner forearm and wait a day.
