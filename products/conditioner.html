Glossing Conditioner

Argan and wheat protein.

A conditioner without silicones. Gentle enough for everyday use.

Key ingredients
  • Shea Butter
  • Argan Oil

How to use
After shampooing, work through mid-lengths to ends. Leave 1–2 minutes, then rinse.
Suited to
Hair that is combed out wet; used as often as the hair is washed.
How to use
After shampooing, work through mid-lengths and ends. Leave one to two minutes, then rinse.
Dosage
A small quantity, distributed from mid-length to tip. Avoid the scalp.
  • Argan kernel oil is composed predominantly of unsaturated fatty acids — oleic acid at roughly 45–48% and linoleic acid at roughly 31% of the fatty-acid fraction.2
  • The oil carries tocopherols, with gamma-tocopherol the predominant vitamer — the molecules to which the oil's antioxidant behaviour is attributed in the literature.2
  • Argan is one of few plant oils reported to carry the phytosterols schottenol and spinasterol; they are the constituents most particular to this species.2
  • Shea butter is a fat of two principal fatty acids, stearic and oleic, alongside an unsaponifiable fraction of tocopherols and phytosterols. The proportion of the two acids varies with the provenance of the butter — stearic-dominant in West African material, oleic-dominant in East African.4
  • Shea butter carries acetyl and cinnamyl polycyclic triterpene esters, reported at country means of roughly 3.7–12.6% of the fat.4
  • The polyphenols of the shea kernel — catechins and gallic acid chief among them — carry only sparingly into the butter itself: a mean of some 97 ppm, against roughly 4000 ppm of catechins in the kernel.5

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Oleic acid
The major fatty acid of argan kernel oil, reported at roughly 45–48% of the fatty-acid fraction; also one of the two principal fatty acids of shea butter.
Linoleic acid
Reported at roughly 31% of the argan kernel oil fatty-acid fraction.
Stearic acid
The other principal fatty acid of shea butter; dominant in West African provenances.
Tocopherols
Gamma-tocopherol predominant in argan kernel oil; tocopherols are also reported in the unsaponifiable fraction of shea butter.
Schottenol and spinasterol
Phytosterols reported in argan kernel oil and near-particular to the species.
Acetyl and cinnamyl triterpene esters
Reported at country means of roughly 3.7–12.6% of shea butter.
Panthenol
Provitamin B5, declared in the INCI.
Hydrolysed wheat protein
A wheat-derived protein hydrolysate, declared in the INCI.

Full ingredients — INCI

Aqua, Cetearyl Alcohol, Butyrospermum Parkii Butter, Argania Spinosa Kernel Oil, Behentrimonium Methosulfate, Glycerin, Panthenol, Hydrolyzed Wheat Protein, Benzyl Alcohol, Dehydroacetic Acid, Parfum.

Vessel
200 mL pump bottle
Material
Stoneware bottle with a pump dispenser.
Recyclability
The stoneware is fired clay — kept and refilled rather than recycled. The pump is a mixed-material mechanism; separate it from the bottle before disposal.

Storage. Closed, away from light and heat.

Precautions. For external use. Avoid the eyes; rinse with water if contact occurs. Allergic contact dermatitis to argan oil has been reported in the published literature — a single patch-test-confirmed case, which establishes that sensitisation is possible but says nothing of its frequency (PMID 25345797). The formula contains a wheat-derived protein hydrolysate, which those with a known wheat sensitivity should take into account. Discontinue if irritation occurs.
