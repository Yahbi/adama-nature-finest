How to use
Massage into hands as needed through the day.
  • Calendula flowers are recognised for their triterpenoid saponins, built on an oleanolic acid backbone. The plant makes both glucuronide and glucoside forms, and they are not evenly distributed: the glucuronides accumulate chiefly in the flowers, the glucosides in the roots.1
  • Targeted profiling of the plant confirms that it accumulates triterpenoids — oleanolic acid and its saponin glycosides — alongside the sterols sitosterol and stigmasterol, carried as free, ester and glycoside conjugates.2
  • The flower has been quantified for phenolic acids, flavonoids and assimilatory pigments — the chlorophylls and carotenoids that account for its colour. How much of each is drawn out depends on the solvent used; methanol recovers flavonoids most efficiently.3
  • Marigold is among the plants traditionally used topically, and a systematic review of 145 references examined it for antibacterial, antifungal and wound-healing activity. The scope is canine dermatology and the underlying evidence is overwhelmingly in vitro; the authors call it a promising candidate that still requires clinical research.4
  • Shea butter, the second botanical in this balm, is recognised for a lipid fraction built principally of triacylglycerols, with stearic and oleic acid the two dominant fatty acids. Which of the two leads is a matter of provenance rather than grade: across 150 butters surveyed from four African countries, stearic led in West African material and oleic in Ugandan.5
  • The same survey reports shea's distinctive unsaponifiable constituents — acetyl and cinnamyl polycyclic triterpene esters — at country means of roughly 3.7 to 12.6 per cent of the fat. It is an unusually large minor fraction for a plant butter.5
  • Crude shea butter is recognised for carrying tocopherols, phytosterols, phenolics and flavonoids within its unsaponifiable matter. Refining lowers those minor components.6

The properties above are drawn from the published literature cited below. They describe the constituents of the ingredients, not a tested effect of this product.

Principal constituents

Triterpenoid saponins (oleanolic acid glycosides)
The characteristic constituent class of the flower; the glucuronide forms accumulate there rather than in the root.
Free triterpenoids — oleanolic acid
The aglycone backbone the flower's saponins are built on.
Phytosterols — β-sitosterol and stigmasterol
Present in the plant as free, ester and glycoside conjugates.
Flavonoids and phenolic acids
Quantified in flower extract; recovery varies with the solvent used.
Carotenoid and chlorophyll pigments
The assimilatory pigments of the flower head.
Triacylglycerols, stearic acid and oleic acid
The bulk of the shea butter. The ratio of the two fatty acids varies with provenance.
Acetyl and cinnamyl polycyclic triterpene esters
Shea's characteristic unsaponifiable constituents; country means of roughly 3.7–12.6 per cent of the fat.
Prunus Amygdalus Dulcis Oil
Sweet almond oil; the second lipid phase of the balm.
Glycerin
Humectant.
Cetearyl Alcohol and Glyceryl Stearate
Fatty alcohol and emulsifier; they hold the emulsion together.
Tocopherol
Vitamin E; here at antioxidant use, protecting the lipid phase.

Full ingredients — INCI

Aqua, Butyrospermum Parkii Butter, Prunus Amygdalus Dulcis Oil, Glycerin, Cetearyl Alcohol, Calendula Officinalis Extract, Glyceryl Stearate, Tocopherol, Benzyl Alcohol, Parfum.

Declarable fragrance allergens

Benzyl Alcohol — declared on the deck and among the 26 substances listed in Annex III of EC 1223/2009. The Parfum declares no further allergens of that list.

Vessel
100 mL tube
Recyclability
Tubes are emptied but rarely rinsed clean, and the cap is usually a different polymer from the sleeve. Squeeze the tube out, replace the cap, and check that your local stream accepts tubes before adding it.

Storage. Keep the tube closed. Store away from direct sunlight and heat.

Precautions. For external use. Avoid the eye area. Calendula is an Asteraceae plant; anyone who reacts to daisies, ragweed, mugwort or chamomile may wish to note it. Sweet almond is a tree nut; those who avoid tree nuts should read the deck. This balm is fragranced. Discontinue if irritation occurs.
